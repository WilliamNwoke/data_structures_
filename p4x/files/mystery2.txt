`You can't think how glad I am to see you again, you dear old thing!' said the Duchess, as she tucked her
arm affectionately into Alice's, and they walked off together. Alice was very glad to find her in such
a pleasant temper, and thought to herself that perhaps it was only the pepper that had made her so savage
when they met in the kitchen. `When I'M a Duchess,' she said to herself, (not in a very hopeful tone though),
`I won't have any pepper in my kitchen AT ALL.  Soup does very well without--Maybe it's always pepper that
makes people hot-tempered,' she went on, very much pleased at having found out a new kind of rule,
